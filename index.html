Step 01 · Two minutes

Which side of economics are you?

Economics has four broad sides: markets and money, people and behaviour, policy and public good, data and forecasting. Five questions. No wrong answers — just an honest starting point.

Step 02 · The map

The pathway, fork by fork.

Most career pages show you destinations. What actually matters at your age are the decision points — where the road forks, what each fork keeps open, and what it quietly closes. Pick your board and read the map that applies to you.

01

The Class 11 choice

End of Class 10 · The one everyone panics about

Under CBSE, economics travels with two streams: Commerce (with or without maths) and Humanities. The single most consequential sub-choice is not the stream. It is whether mathematics comes with you.

Under IB, you choose Economics at HL or SL alongside your other five subjects. The consequential pairing is Economics with Maths AA or AI, and at which level — universities read that combination closely.

Economics + MathsCommerce with maths / Humanities + mathsEcon HL + Maths AA (HL or SL) +

Keeps open: BSc Economics at serious departments, econometrics, data science, actuarial science, quant finance, ISI and IIT-style entrances, and every economics master's programme worth its name. Real economics at university is a mathematical discipline — this fork is what makes you eligible for it.

Costs you: The hardest two years of the two options. If maths is a genuine daily struggle rather than mere dislike, forcing this fork can wreck your Class 12 score, which matters more for admission than the combination does.

CBSE note: Applied Mathematics (241) does not count as core maths for many BSc Economics programmes, ISI, or engineering-adjacent routes. If you want the full menu, take Mathematics (041).

IB note: For economics, finance and data-leaning degrees, Maths AA signals more strongly than AI at most selective universities — LSE, for instance, states a preference for AA at HL for its Economics BSc. AI SL closes some doors AA keeps open.

Economics without MathsCommerce (no maths) / HumanitiesEcon SL/HL + Maths AI SL +

Keeps open: BA Economics at most Indian universities, BBA, B.Com, law (economics + CLAT is a strong pairing), policy, journalism, management, civil services. This is a perfectly good road — millions walk it well.

Costs you: The quantitative wing of the subject. Most rigorous BSc Economics programmes, actuarial science, and quant roles will be closed or require painful catch-up later. Several DU colleges also ask for Class 12 maths for BA (Hons) Economics — check the current CUET requirement before assuming.

Honest rule of thumb: drop maths because of a considered plan, never because of one bad exam in Class 10.

Decision point · Ask yourself

"Am I choosing away from maths, or towards something?" Choosing away is how students end up in Class 12 with strong marks and no direction. Choosing towards — law, writing, policy, business — makes the no-maths fork a strategy instead of an escape.

FORK
02

The degree fork

Class 12 · Where the applications actually go

Four families of degrees absorb most economics students. They are not interchangeable, and the names matter less than what each one trains you to do daily.

BA / BSc Economics (Hons)The core route+

The full discipline: micro, macro, econometrics, development. In India the strongest departments include DSE-affiliated DU colleges, Ashoka, ISI (BStat/BEcon route), Madras School of Economics, and Shiv Nadar; abroad, economics is among the most competitive majors everywhere.

Best for: research, policy, analytics, master's abroad, UPSC with economics optional. Watch out: a pass-course BA Economics from a weak department, with no internships and no data skills, is one of India's most common underemployment traps. The degree is a platform, not a destination.

Admission reality: CUET domain tests + high board pressure for DU; private universities (Ashoka, Krea, FLAME) run their own holistic rounds.

Admission reality: IB predicted grades carry the application for foreign universities; for Indian private universities your IB scores convert cleanly, while DU requires CUET like everyone else.

B.Com / BBA / ManagementThe commerce route+

Accounting, finance, and business operations with economics as a supporting subject. Pairs naturally with CA, CS, CFA or an MBA later. IPMAT (IIM Indore/Rohtak five-year programmes) is the ambitious version of this fork straight from Class 12.

Best for: corporate finance, accounting, family business, entrepreneurship. Watch out: this fork trades depth in economics for breadth in business — moving back into pure economics later is harder than the reverse.

Economics + Data / CSThe 2030 route+

Joint or double-major programmes: economics with data science, statistics or computer science. Plaksha's Data Science, Economics & Business BTech, Ashoka's econ + CS combinations, and abroad the classic Econ/CS double major. This is where the strongest early-career salaries in the economics family currently sit.

Best for: data science, quant research, product, fintech. Requires: the maths fork behind you. This door does not open without it.

Economics + Law / Liberal ArtsThe words-and-institutions route+

Five-year integrated law (economics is the single most useful CLAT companion subject), or liberal arts degrees with an economics major. Economics + law feeds competition law, arbitration and regulatory practice — among the best-paid corners of Indian law.

Best for: law, policy, journalism, civil services. Watch out: liberal arts fees at private universities are significant; check scholarship policy before falling in love.

FORK
03

The 22-year-old fork

After the degree · Where routes become careers

Nobody expects you to decide this now. But seeing it now changes how you spend your college years — because each of these rewards a different thing you can start building early.

Straight to workAnalyst roles · consulting · finance+

Banks, consulting firms, research houses and startups hire economics graduates as analysts. Campus matters enormously here — the same degree from different colleges produces very different first offers. What levels the field: internships, Excel-to-Python data skills, and evidence you can write clearly.

Master's / researchMA, MSc, MPhil, PhD+

The credential route: DSE, ISI, JNU, or abroad. An economics master's is the entry ticket to being called an economist — at the RBI, in think tanks, in international organisations. Econometrics grades and maths rigour from your bachelor's decide who gets in.

Professional examsMBA · UPSC · IES · RBI Grade B · CFA+

India's exam layer sits on top of every degree. The Indian Economic Service (IES) is the specialist civil service for economists; RBI Grade B is among the most sought-after regulator jobs; CAT-to-MBA remains the classic salary reset. All are brutal ratios — treat them as plans, not backups.

Build somethingEntrepreneurship+

Economics is arguably the best founder's degree that isn't engineering: pricing, incentives, unit economics and market structure are the daily language of running a company. No gate, no exam — just the highest variance of every route on this page.

Careers you didn't know came from economics.

Sport

Sports Economist

IPL auction strategy, ticket pricing, salary caps and match-fixing detection are all applied economics. Franchises and leagues hire for exactly this.

Climate

Climate Economist

Carbon pricing, green bonds and climate risk models — one of the fastest-growing specialisations of the decade, in India and globally.

Tech

Marketplace Designer

Someone decides how surge pricing works, how ads are auctioned, how creators get paid. Tech firms hire economists to design these markets.

Crime

Forensic Economist

Courts need someone to put a number on damages — lost earnings, cartel overcharges, fraud. Forensic economists are those expert witnesses.

Mind

Behavioural Scientist

Why do people skip insurance and fall for sales? Banks, governments and health programmes employ behavioural teams to design around human nature.

Story

Economic Journalist

The person who explains a budget or a crash to a hundred million readers. Economics graduates run the business press — and the best explainers on YouTube.

Step 04 · Zero sugar-coating

The parts nobody puts on a poster.

You deserve the hard numbers at 15, not at 21. Each truth comes with the honest way through it.

Top colleges are genuinely brutal to enter.

BA (Hons) Economics at the top DU colleges routinely demands near-perfect CUET performance, and Ashoka-tier private universities reject most applicants. The way through: apply in tiers, and remember strong departments exist well beyond the five famous names.

A BA in economics is not, by itself, a job.

India graduates lakhs of economics students every year; the ones who struggle are almost always degree-only profiles. The way through: two internships, one data skill (Excel → Python or R), and proof you can write. That trio beats a famous college name without them.

The maths decision is nearly one-way.

Dropping maths in Class 11 is easy; re-adding its consequences at 20 is not. Quant routes, ISI, and most serious master's programmes assume it. The way through: if you're unsure, keep maths and decide later — that option has value, like everything else in economics.

Exam-layer ratios are astronomical.

RBI Grade B, IES and UPSC see lakhs of applicants for a few hundred seats. Treating them as a casual backup is how years disappear. The way through: decide deliberately, prepare with a time limit, and keep a parallel track alive.

First salaries vary wildly by campus.

The same analyst title can pay ₹4L at one college's placement and ₹20L+ at another's. That gap is real and unfair. The way through: the gap shrinks sharply by year five for people who compound skills — the entry point is not the ceiling.

Economics ≠ finance.

Plenty of students pick economics wanting money careers and meet philosophy, statistics and development instead. The way through: read one real economics book before Class 11 (library below). If it bores you completely, that's data — use it.

Step 05 · Go deeper

The library: read, watch, take.

Ten of anything beats forty of everything. Each pick earns its place — the one line tells you why.

01
Freakonomics
Levitt & Dubner

The gateway drug. Shows economics is a way of asking questions about anything — sumo wrestlers, baby names, crime.

02
The Undercover Economist
Tim Harford

The clearest explanation of how markets actually work, told through coffee shops and supermarkets. Read before Class 11.

03
Poor Economics
Abhijit Banerjee & Esther Duflo

Nobel-winning work on why poverty persists — with half its evidence from India. Development economics at its most human.

04
Misbehaving
Richard Thaler

The funny, rebellious history of behavioural economics — how one field proved humans aren't calculators.

05
Thinking, Fast and Slow
Daniel Kahneman

Not strictly economics — the psychology underneath it. Dense; read slowly; changes how you see your own decisions.

06
Doughnut Economics
Kate Raworth

The strongest challenge to everything else on this list. Read it to learn that economics argues with itself — that's the fun.

07
Good Economics for Hard Times
Banerjee & Duflo

Immigration, trade, jobs, growth — what the evidence really says about the arguments in your living room.

08
The Armchair Economist
Steven Landsburg

Short, provocative essays that train the economist's reflex: always ask "compared to what?"

01
CrashCourse Economics
YouTube · 35 episodes

The entire Class 11–12 syllabus, funnier and faster. The single best starting point.

02
Economics Explained
YouTube

Whole countries as case studies — why Argentina keeps defaulting, how Singapore got rich. Macro made watchable.

03
Marginal Revolution University
mru.org · free

University-grade micro and macro from Tyler Cowen and Alex Tabarrok, in five-minute pieces.

04
Everything is Everything
Podcast · Ajay Shah & Amit Varma

Two of India's sharpest minds on the Indian economy. Long episodes; pick topics you care about.

05
TED-Ed economics playlist
YouTube

Five-minute animated answers to questions like "what causes inflation?" Perfect between classes.

06
Money & Macro
YouTube

A PhD economist fact-checks the hype cycles — crypto, de-dollarisation, crashes. Teaches healthy scepticism.

01
The Economy FREE
CORE Econ

The modern open textbook-course used by universities worldwide. Start Unit 1 in Class 11 and you're ahead of most first-years.

02
Khan Academy Micro + Macro FREE
khanacademy.org

Maps almost exactly onto CBSE and IB syllabi. Use it as your second teacher all through Class 11–12.

03
Financial Markets
Yale / Robert Shiller · Coursera

A Nobel laureate teaching how markets and human psychology collide. Audit free; certificate optional.

04
Principles of Economics FREE
MIT OpenCourseWare 14.01

Real MIT lectures, problem sets included. For the student who wants to test-drive university economics honestly.

05
International Economics Olympiad (IEO)
School-nominated · Classes 9–12

The proof layer: a genuine olympiad with an Indian national round. Ask your counsellor about nomination.

06
Data skills starter: Python for Everybody FREE
University of Michigan · Coursera

Not an economics course — the skill that doubles the value of every economics degree. Start in Class 11 summer.
